Course Overview

The Big Idea

Classic literature isn't locked in the past.

The themes of Othello — manipulation, toxic power dynamics, racism, and sexism — are still happening today. By translating Shakespeare into modern contexts and changing the narrative, we can take away the villain's power and rewrite the tragedy.

Learning Goals & Success Criteria

Learning Goals

  • Analyze how characters use manipulation, language, and gaslighting to gain power over others.
  • Examine how societal biases (racism, prejudice) affect the characters in the play.
  • Effectively communicate ideas and debate with peers using modern digital tools.
  • Analyze power dynamics and societal biases; creatively adapt Shakespearean text into modern language.

Success Criteria — I can…

  • Identify and explain specific quotes where Iago plants "fake news" in other characters' minds.
  • Explain how Othello's identity as a Black man in a white society makes him vulnerable to manipulation.
  • Record a clear, thoughtful video or audio response to a discussion prompt.
  • Thoughtfully reply to a peer, respectfully challenging or adding to their ideas.
  • Translate Elizabethan English into modern slang and perform a soliloquy that subverts toxic themes.

Week 5 · Final Summative

The Soliloquy Twist

Take back the narrative. Rewrite the tragedy.

The Assignment

Step 1

Choose one famous soliloquy or monologue from the play — Iago plotting his revenge, Othello in his jealousy, Emilia speaking about how men treat women.

Step 2

Translate the speech entirely into modern language. Slang is encouraged!

Step 3 — The Twist

Change the ending so the character makes a different choice — stopping racism, sexism, or bullying in its tracks. What if Othello realized Iago was gaslighting him? What if Emilia stood up earlier?

Step 4

Record a 2–3 minute video of yourself performing your modern, twisted soliloquy. TikTok-style editing, props, or costumes are welcome!
